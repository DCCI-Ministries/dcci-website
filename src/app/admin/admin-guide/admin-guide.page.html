Admin Guide

How to use the admin area

This guide is for ministry staff who manage the website day to day. You do not need to be technical — it explains what each part of the dashboard does and how to work safely.

Who can access the dashboard?

No one gets admin access by signing up alone. That is deliberate — it protects the website.

  • Someone may create an account on the admin login page, but they start as Pending with no access to the dashboard.
  • Only a current Super Admin or Admin can open User Management and assign a role.
  • Roles: Super Admin (Hatun — full access), Admin (most tools), Moderator (limited), User (no dashboard — future comments).
  • Until that happens, they cannot open the dashboard even with the correct password.
  • They must also verify their email before they can sign in as an admin.

If you need to add a helper, ask them to sign up (if they have not already), then go to Quick Actions → User Management and assign their role. Do not share your password.

Signing in
  1. Use the admin login page your developer gave you.
  2. Enter the email address an admin has approved for you.
  3. Enter your password.
  4. If asked, verify your email — you cannot use admin tools until that is done.
  5. If your role is Super Admin, Admin, or Moderator, you arrive on the Admin Dashboard.

“Access denied” after login usually means your account is still Pending — ask an existing admin to approve you in User Management.

User roles — what each level can do
Pending
Signed up but not approved. No dashboard access.
User
Future public accounts (profiles, article comments). No dashboard access today.
Moderator
Limited dashboard — mainly YouTube settings. Most Quick Actions are hidden.
Admin
Most dashboard tools: content, welcome page, user management, emergency controls. (May be narrowed later.)
Super Admin
Full access to everything. Hatun should hold this role.

Only Super Admin or Admin can assign roles in User Management.

 Recent Activity

A short list of what happened lately — articles published, contact forms received, newsletter sign-ups, and draft edits. It helps you see that the site is active. It does not show the text of contact form messages (for privacy).

Overall Usage

Shows how much space the website is using on our hosting plan (images, articles, and settings). Yellow or red bars mean you are getting close to the free limit — tell your developer if that happens.

Editing the Welcome Page (safe workflow)

Changes do not go live until you publish. Visitors keep seeing the old page until then.

  1. Open Quick Actions → Welcome Page.
  2. Edit text, images, social links, or support links.
  3. Click Save draft — safe to leave and come back later.
  4. Click Preview — opens how the page will look (yellow banner at top; not public yet).
  5. When happy, click Publish live — visitors see the new page; Google’s copy updates shortly after.

Made a mistake? Scroll to Previous versions on the Welcome Page screen. You can load an older version into the editor or publish it again (up to 10 saved versions).

Discard draft throws away unsaved work in the editor and reloads what is currently live.

 Contact form messages
  • Messages are emailed directly to your ministry inbox — not stored as full text in the dashboard.
  • Use Reply in your email app to answer genuine visitors.
  • At the bottom of each contact form email there are links to report spam or ask your developer a question.
  • If the Messages count goes up but you did not receive email, contact your website developer.
 What this dashboard cannot do
  • Read contact form message bodies (by design — for visitor privacy).
  • Change your ministry email address — that is a developer or email-provider task.
  • Fix email delivery or hosting — contact your website developer.
 Need help?

For website problems, email your website developer (the technical contact for this site). Use subject line Urgent: Hatun Website Question if it is urgent (site down, form broken, security worry).

For normal ministry replies to visitors, use your email app — not the developer.
